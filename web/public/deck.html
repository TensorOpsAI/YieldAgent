Launch real ad campaigns by talking to an agent. It plans, predicts, and waits for your yes.

2026 · live draft on a real ad account · human approves every write
01 · The problem

Setting up campaigns is slow, manual, and easy to get wrong.

  • 01Death by form fields. One campaign means clicking through dozens of settings in a clunky ad manager.
  • 02Every platform is its own dialect. What you learned on LinkedIn does not carry to Meta or Google.
  • 03Real money, real mistakes. One wrong toggle and budget burns on the wrong audience.
  • 04Review comes too late. The senior check usually happens after the campaign is already live.
02 · Solution & architecture

One agent. Any platform. Human in the loop.

  • APlain language in. It reads the real platform rules, taxonomy, and audience size. It never guesses a target.
  • BA full proposal out. Creative preview, estimated reach, and a results forecast that matches Campaign Manager.
  • CYou hold the trigger. Nothing is written before you approve. It only ever creates a draft, so it never spends on its own.

03 · Live demo

What you are about to see.

  • 1One sentence, one campaign. LinkedIn brand awareness across Portugal, Israel, and the UK, sponsoring an existing post, with budget and flight dates inline.
  • 2It works in the open. Watch it pick the platform, read the rules, resolve Director+ in Marketing and Engineering, and size the audience step by step.
  • 3The proposal. Preview of the sponsored post, estimated reach, and a forecast of spend, impressions, and CTR over the 10–24 Jun window.
  • 4Your call. Approve and it creates a draft on the real ad account. Reject and it revises. Going live is still a manual click.
04 · The road ahead

Where this goes next.

  • →More platforms, zero agent changes. Meta and Google drop in behind the same connector contract.
  • →No API? Drive the UI. Today the connectors call ad APIs. A Playwright connector can drive the ad manager directly for platforms that gate or lack an API.
  • →True multi platform plans. One intent, separate budgets and targeting per platform, in a single approval.
  • →From creation to optimization. The forecast loop becomes a recommendation loop.
  • →Guardrails as a feature. Draft only, allowlists, approval gates, and an audit trail by default.
  • →Open source on GitHub. The whole project lives at github.com/TensorOpsAI/YieldAgent — anyone can read it, fork it, and contribute a connector or a role.
You stay in control. The agent does the clicking.
